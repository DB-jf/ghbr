ABOUT CGHBR

Advancing health through ethical research, capacity building and trusted consultancy

We partner with governments, institutions and researchers across Africa to design and deliver practical, ethics-centered and evidence-based research that improves health outcomes.

Who We Are

Centre for Global Health & Bioethics Research (CGHBR) provides independent, high-quality research and consultancy services. We help private organisations and institutions in the public sector make evidence-based decisions that improve health outcomes. We provide relevant data through rigorous methodology, guided by relevant ethical principles to ensure equity. The centre was established in 2024 in Ghana, and we also provide services such as policy evaluation, institutional capacity building, and hands-on bioethics training.

Leadership

Israel Kwame Kesse Adjei

Chief Executive Officer & Founder

Israel Kwame Kesse Adjei is the Chief Executive Officer and founder of the Centre for Global Health & Bioethics Research Consultancy Ltd, Ghana. He has over 15 years of experience as a researcher and serves as a deputy chief nutritionist with the Ghana Health Service (GHS), Bono East Regional Health Directorate. He has led research across maternal health, vaccine development, bioethics, and non-communicable diseases, and teaches as an adjunct faculty in the School of Public Health. He recently submitted his PhD thesis in Public Health at the University of Ghana.

Mission & What We Do

Our Mission

To strengthen health systems and research conduct through practical, ethics-centered research, training and advisory services.

Core activities

  • Design and implement maternal health studies
  • Provide IRB support, ethics reviews and research integrity guidance
  • Deliver tailored training and toolkits for ethics committees
  • Translate evidence into actionable policy recommendations

Featured Projects & Publications

On-going: Bioethicists in Ghana’s Workforce

The study titled "Bioethicists in Ghana’s Workforce: Situational Analysis and Perspectives of Ghanaian Stakeholders on Employment Opportunities for Bioethicists in Ghana" is a qualitative situational analysis conducted as part of the NYU–University of Ghana Research Integrity Training Program (RITP). This project explores employment opportunities and stakeholder perspectives. Funded by NYU–UG RITP.

On-going: Assessing the Economic & Socio-Cultural Impact of Chale Wote on James Town

This study investigates why James Town was not the eventual location for the Chale Wote Street Arts Festival and examines the festival's socio-economic and cultural effects on the community from stakeholders’ perspectives.

Completed: Mobile health intervention — 'Contra Health'

Quasi-experimental study assessing the effect of a mobile SRH application ("Contra Health") on contraceptive use and discontinuation among women of reproductive age at two government hospitals. The study tested effectiveness on uptake and discontinuation rates.

Publication: Advance Directives in Ghana

Advance directives in Ghana: a content analysis of three Ghanaian policy documents and their implications for dementia care. Advance directives are statements written in advance to preserve a person’s medical wishes. This study shows limited incorporation of directives across Ghanaian policy documents and discusses implications for dementia care.

Authors: Kwame K. Adjei, Irene Adjei, Linda, Isaac Boadu
