Research & Publications

Our work strengthens institutions, advances bioethics, promotes responsible scientific inquiry, and supports public health improvements across Ghana and beyond.

Who We Are

The Centre for Global Health & Bioethics Research (GHBR) provides independent, high-quality research and consultancy services. We support both private and public institutions in making evidence-based decisions that improve health outcomes, strengthen systems, and advance ethical research practices.

Featured Projects & Publications

Bioethicists in Ghana’s Workforce

A qualitative national study exploring employment pathways for bioethicists in Ghana under the NYU–University of Ghana Research Integrity Training Program (RITP).

Socio-Cultural & Economic Impact of Chale Wote Festival

An assessment of community perceptions, cultural outcomes, and economic implications of relocating the Chale Wote Street Art Festival away from James Town.

Contra Health — Mobile App Study on Contraceptive Use

A quasi-experimental evaluation of a reproductive health mobile application examining contraceptive uptake and discontinuation trends among women in Ghana.

Study Type: Digital Health / mHealth Intervention

Latest News & Insight

Advance Directives in Ghana — Policy Analysis

A detailed review of national health policy documents to determine the presence and role of advance directives, with implications for dementia and end-of-life care.

Read more at: Medical Humanities Journal

World Congress on Research Integrity — Vancouver 2026

GHBR researchers will participate in the 2026 WCRI event, engaging with global leaders in research integrity, policy reform, and ethical governance.
