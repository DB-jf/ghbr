Research & Publications

Our work focuses on ethical, evidence-based research that supports public health, strengthens institutions, and advances bioethics across Ghana and beyond.

Who We Are

Centre for Global Health & Bioethics Research (CGHBR) provides independent, high-quality research and consultancy services. We help private organisations and public sector institutions make evidence-based decisions that improve health outcomes. Established in 2024 in Ghana, our work is grounded in rigorous methodology and guided by ethical principles to ensure equity. We also offer policy evaluation, institutional capacity building, and hands-on bioethics training.

Featured Projects & Publications

Bioethicists in Ghana’s Workforce

This qualitative study examines employment opportunities for bioethicists in Ghana as part of the New York University–University of Ghana Research Integrity Training Program (RITP). The study is fully funded by the NYU–UG RITP and aims to provide insight into strengthening the bioethics workforce.

Economic & Socio-Cultural Impact of the Chale Wote Festival

This study investigates why James Town — a culturally rich community — no longer serves as the venue for the Chale Wote Street Art Festival. It further explores the socio-economic and cultural impact of the festival from the perspective of community stakeholders.

Impact of ‘Contra Health’ Mobile Application on Contraceptive Use

This quasi-experimental study evaluated the effects of a sexual and reproductive health mobile app on contraceptive use among women at Shai-Osudoku Hospital. The research also assessed discontinuation rates among app users.

Type: Mobile App Intervention Study
